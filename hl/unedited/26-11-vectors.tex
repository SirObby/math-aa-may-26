AB vectors has initial point A and terminal point B, even though a vector does not have a location.

Opposite vector of AB is BA=-AB has same magnitude and opposite direction

triangle law: AB+BC=AC
parallel vector = collinear, $a||b$

$t<0$ opposite direction
so magnitude changes, same angle.
vecors in 3-dimensions:
OP=$x\cdot i+y\cdot j+z\cdot k$
where i and j and k are unit vectors with x,y,z-axis and P(x,y,z) and O(0,0,0)
Vector between points $A=(x_a,_a,z_a)$ amd $B=(x_b,y_b,z_b)$
Vector AB=$(x_b,x_a)i+(x_b-y_a)j+(z_b-z_a)k$
and magnitude
$|AB|=\sqrt{(x_b-x_a)^2+(y_b-y_a)^2+(z_b-z_a)^2}$
unit vector $\frac{AB}{|AB|}$ has magnitude 1 and direction same as AB.
Example a) Find a vector between $A=(1,2,3)$ and $B=(-3,7,6)$
$AB=\begin{matrix}-3-1\\ 7-2 \\ 6-3\end{matrix}=\begin{matrix}-4\\ 5 \\ 3\end{matrix})-4i+5j+3k$
