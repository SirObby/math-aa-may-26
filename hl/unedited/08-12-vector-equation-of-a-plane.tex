\subsection{Vector equation of a plane}
\obeylines

Plane passing points A,B and C.
has direction vectors AB and AC.
equation of plane$=OA+\lambda\cdot AB+\my AC$
or 
crossproduct gives normal and cartessian equation of plane is
$ax+by+cz+d=0$
where a,b,c are factors of normal=crossproduct and d comes from substitution of any point from plane.
Example. A plae passes points $A=(1,2,3)$, $B=(4,6,8)$ and $C=(1,1,1$
a) Find equation of plane in vector form. 
direction vectors $AB=\begin{vmatrix}4-1 \\ 6-2 \\ 8-3\end{vmatrix}=\begin{vmatrix}3 \\ 4 \\ 5\end{vmatrix}$ 
and AC=$\begin{vmatrix}0 \\ -1 \\ -2\end{vmatrix}$
vector form of plane=$OA+\lambda AB+\my AC$
$=\begin{vmatrix}1\\2\\3\end{vmatrix}+\lambda\begin{vmatrix}3 \\4 \\5 \end{vmatrix}+\my \begin{vmatrix}0 \\ -1 \\ -2\end{vmatrix}$
b) Find the parametric form of the plane
$\begin{cases}x=1+3\lambda+0\my\\y=2+4\lambda+-1\my\\z=3+5\lambda-2\mu\end{cases}$
c) Find the cartesian form of the plane
$ax+by+cz+d=0$ and $a,b,c$ from normal of plane crossproduct
$AB\times AC=\begin{vmatrix}i j k\\ 3 4 5\\ 0 -1 -2\end{vmatrix}$
$=-8i-3k+5i+6j=-3i+6j-3k$
plane $-3x+6y-3z+d=0$
subst C=(1,1,1)
$-3\cdot 1+6\cdot 1-3\cdot 1+d=0$
$d=0$ ans: plane is $-3x+6y-3z+0=0$
