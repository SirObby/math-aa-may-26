line 1. $\begin{cases}t=\frac{x-2}{3}\\t=y\\t=1-z\end{cases}$
-> $\begin{vmatrix}x=2+3t\\y=0+1\cdot t\\z=1-1\cdot t\end{vmatrix}$ has direction vector $3i+1j-1k$
line 2: $\begin{vmatrix}p=x\\p=2y\\p=z-3\end{vmatrix}$

now $\begin{vmatrix}3\\1\\-1\end{vmatrix}\cdot\begin{vmatrix}1\\\frac{1}{2}\\1\end{vmatrix}=3\cdot1+1\cdot1/2+(-1)\cdot1)=3+0.5-1=2.5$
magnitudes: $|3i+1j-1k|=\sqrt{3^2+1^2+(-1)^2}=\sqrt{11}$
$|1i+1/2k+1k|=\sqrt{1^2+(1/2)^2+1^2}=\sqrt{2\frac{1}{4}}=\sqrt{\frac{9}{4}}=\frac{3}{2}$
