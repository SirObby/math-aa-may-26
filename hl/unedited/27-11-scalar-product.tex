\subsection{Scalar product}
p 597
\obeylines
= also dot product=inner product.
$a\cdot b=|a|\cdot |b|cos(\alpha)$ is a numerical value.
Where $\alpha$ is angle between vectors a and b.
$cos(\alpha)=\frac{a\cdot b}{|a||b|}$
for perpendicular vectors $a\cdot b=0$

Example.
a) Find the scalar product of $a=i-2j+4k$ and $b=-21i+6j+7k$
$a\cdot b=1\cdot (-21)+(-2)6+4\cdot7=-21-12+28=-5$
obtuce angle ($>90\degree$ since negative value)
b) Find the angle.
$cos(\alpha)=\frac{a\cdot b}{|a||b|}$
$nSolve(cos(x)=\frac{-5}{\sqrt{1^2+(-2)^2+4^2}\cdot \sqrt{(-21)^2+6^2+7^2}},x)$
$\alpha=92.7\degree (3.sf)$
properties:
1) $a\cdot b=0$ for perpendicular $a\perp b$
2) $a\cdot b=|a|\cdot |b|$ for parallel $a||b$
(or $a=t\cdot b$ for $a||b$)
3) $a\cdot a=a^2$ same vector, coincident.
4) $a\cdot b>0$ then $\alpha<90$ acute
 $a\cdot b<0$ then $\alpha>90$ obtuse.
5) $a\cdot b=b\cdot a$
6) $u\cdot (a+b)=u\cdot a+u\cdot b$
